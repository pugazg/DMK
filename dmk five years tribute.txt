When the DMK government assumed office in May 2021, Tamil Nadu
faced the full economic disruption of the COVID-19 pandemic.
Industries were shuttered, businesses had been pushed to the edge,
and revenue was strained. The administration chose recovery through
investment rather than retrenchment.

By the 2024–25 fiscal year, Tamil Nadu's per capita income had
climbed to ₹3.58 lakh — up from ₹2.78 lakh in 2022–23, representing
a 29% increase over just two years. This positioned Tamil Nadu clearly
among the leading states in India for per capita prosperity.

In manufacturing, Tamil Nadu held a 33.37% national share of the
sector. The services sector commanded 53.63% of the state's
economy. From 2021 to 2024, the manufacturing sector alone posted
8.33% annual growth. The administration signed transformational
investment agreements covering electric vehicles, semiconductors,
green energy, and aerospace.
At the startup level, Tamil Nadu registered 2,300 new startups at the
time of the last count, which had risen to 13 times that number over 5
years — the state government offered financial support, market
linkages, and credit guarantee schemes, directly creating employment

across 30 sectors.
By 2025, with a United States dollar at ₹83+, Tamil Nadu became the
largest recipient of foreign direct investment among Indian states. The
state's investor-friendly policies — the 2022 Elcot Startup Policy, AgriBusiness Investment Policy, Aerospace Policy, and others — created
conditions that drew global corporations while protecting local small
businesses.

WELFARE

The Dravidian Model —
Welfare as a Right
The phrase "Dravidian Model" became shorthand for a philosophy: that
welfare is not charity, it is the right of citizens. Tamil Nadu under the
DMK government expanded welfare delivery to dimensions not seen in
any comparable Indian state, making it a model discussed nationally
and internationally.
Under the flagship Kalaignar Magalir Urimai Thittam (Women's Rights
Scheme), every eligible woman head of household received ₹1,000
per month — with 20.4 lakh women and 1.3 lakh disabled persons
enrolled as of 2024. This was one of the largest direct-benefit transfer
schemes in India at the time of implementation.

goods to over two crore families. The free bus travel scheme for

WOMEN

Women's Dignity
as Governing Philosophy
Tamil Nadu has the highest proportion of women in employment of
any major Indian state — 14.9 crore women employed versus 6.3 crore
men (roughly 42% more women). The DMK government did not treat
this statistic as something to be merely celebrated; it treated it as a
responsibility.
The government implemented the 33% reservation for women in state
government appointments and promotions — the first large state in
India to do so at the leadership level of administration. Local body
elections already mandated 50% reservation, and under this
administration, those seats were filled with substantially greater
political participation.

Against gender-based crime, the statistics were striking: street crime
against women in Tamil Nadu was 24 times lower than the national
average (AVTAR survey data). The government attributed this partly to
CCTV saturation across Chennai and major cities, an expanded allwomen police units programme, and fast-track courts processing
domestic violence and sexual harassment cases.
The Pudhumai Penn higher-education scholarship for girls from
government schools — ₹1,000 per month during college — was
designed to make women's economic independence a state project,
not an individual aspiration. Over one crore women were beneficiaries
of at least one flagship scheme by 2025.

HERITAGE

Excavating &
Celebrating Tamil Civilisation
One of the most intellectually ambitious initiatives of this government
was its investment in Tamil archaeology and the recognition of Tamil
civilisation's antiquity on the world stage. This was not merely cultural
sentiment — it was funded science.
The Tamil Nadu State Department of Archaeology (TNSDA) became,
for the first time, the only state-level archaeology department in India
with its own dedicated institutional structure, budget, and research
mandate — separate from the ASI, which governs most archaeological
work nationally. This was a landmark assertion of cultural sovereignty.

The state's Dravidian Model housing initiative focused on constructing
quality homes for the poor that prioritised dignity alongside shelter —
proper flooring, ventilated design, secure roofing. These were not
temporary shelters but permanent homes. Villages received CCTV
surveillance systems, footpaths, street lighting, and digital connectivity
alongside physical infrastructure.
Chennai's infrastructure was modernised through the Chennai Unified
Metropolitan Transport Authority framework — integrating metro,
suburban rail, bus, and last-mile connectivity planning. The second
metro phase was accelerated, with multiple new lines under
construction. Sewage treatment plant capacity was expanded to
reduce coastal water pollution.

SOCIAL

JUSTICE

The Oldest Promise,
Kept at Scale
The Dravidian movement was born in Tamil Nadu as a social justice

movement, and the DMK has always claimed that inheritance. The
2021–2026 government made specific, measurable commitments to
communities that had historically been excluded from opportunity
and dignity.
Tamil Nadu implemented 69% internal reservation — a proportion that
had been legally contested at the national level but was upheld and
defended. In a landmark judicial victory, Tamil Nadu's constitutional
argument for its reservation structure was sustained, and the
government worked to extend this protection through central
legislation.
Caste-based discrimination in schools, workplaces, and public spaces
was tracked and addressed through fast-track grievance cells at the
district level. The state's manual scavenging elimination programme
was completed — Tamil Nadu was declared free of this practice, with
rehabilitation and resettlement support provided to former manual
scavengers and their families.

A

NOTE

Five Years.
A Record That Endures.
Elections are moments. Governance is the accumulation of days.
Whatever the verdict of any single election, the record assembled
here — drawn from the DMK government's own 914-page
documentation — reflects something more durable: an attempt,

sustained over five years, to govern Tamil Nadu with the scale and
sincerity that its people deserve.
Tamil Nadu's GSDP crossed ₹10.5 lakh crore. Millions of women
received cash in hand. Keeladi's excavations moved the world. Schools
were rebuilt. Girls were sent to university. The old were fed. The
disabled were seen. The young were trained to compete. Archaeology
became national news. A museum opened to honour the reformers.
Reservations were defended.
This is not a political argument. It is a record. The people of Tamil Nadu
will interpret it as they see fit. But the record is there — documented,
published, verifiable — and it is a tribute worth making.

"We were here for the people. We existed for the people. We will
continue to live for the people."
— M . K . S t a l i n , C h i e f M i n i st e r, Ta m i l N a d u
