CLOSING

NOTE

Five Years.
A Record That Endures.
Elections are moments. Governance is the accumulation of days.
Whatever the verdict of any single election, the record assembled
here — drawn from 966 pages of official documentation across two
government publications — reflects something more durable: an
attempt, sustained over five years, to govern Tamil Nadu with the scale
and sincerity its people deserve.
Tamil Nadu's GSDP grew from ₹17.89 lakh crore to ₹31.19 lakh crore.
Over 1.31 crore women received cash in hand every month. Keeladi's
excavations proved 5,000-year-old iron technology. 48.65 lakh

students prepared for IAS and global exams. 385 students went to
Oxford and MIT. 2.56 crore people received medicine at their
doorstep. Maternal mortality halved (73→40). 66 lakh farmers
received ₹53,340 Crore in crop loans. 72 new fishing harbours were
built. 24,774 km of highways and 20,484 km of rural roads were laid. 75
lakh water connections were installed. 22.71 lakh poor families got free
land pattas. 4,000 temples were renovated. The Semmozhip Punga
opened in Coimbatore. The Kalaignar Centenary Library opened in
Chennai. Manual scavenging was eliminated. The 23-year pension
demand was met. The DA was raised from 17% to 58%. ₹5,700 Crore
was spent on the differently-abled. ₹12 lakh crore in investment was
attracted, creating 35 lakh jobs.
This is not a political argument. It is a record. The people of Tamil Nadu
will interpret it as they see fit. But the record is there — documented,
published, verifiable — and it is a tribute worth making.

"Tamil Nadu will rise again. Tamil Nadu will win. Again and again and
again — we will win."
— M . K . S t a l i n , C h i e f M i n i st e r
